League Information

Mandate

The mandate of the Nortel Networks Mixed Softball League (the Softball League) is to provide a safe and fun league where players of all skill levels can play recreational softball.

 

Eligibility Requirements

Player Eligibility

Anyone who has reached the age of 16 by the date for the first scheduled game of a season, is eligible to play in the league.

Team Registration

To be included in the playing schedule, teams must be registered with the league during the registration period. The registration period is nominally the last week in March and the first two weeks in April. For the new season’s specific dates and team registration instructions go to the league's web registration page.  Each team must have three unique contacts with daytime email addresses and phone numbers where they can be reached by other team contacts.  It is not necessary that contacts are Nortel Networks’ employees, but it is helpful if one team contact has Nortel Networks building access to aid in the pickup and return of equipment.

Tier assignment

Teams are assigned to tiers based on the assessment of the league executive.  The goal of the tier placement is to have the same number of teams in each tier and to balance the level of play in each tier to provide for competitive yet enjoyable games.  Tier placement is judged by several factors, including the team's requested tier, the number of teams of the same approximate skill level, and (in the case of returning teams), the team’s previous year’s performance and the number of returning players.

Roster Requirements

A team’s electronic roster must include at least 15 players with a minimum of four of each sex.  For player registration instructions, see the league's web registration page.

Indemnity Form

All players must have signed the indemnity form before they play a league game.  The indemnity form is a legal document.  Adult players must sign the form personally.  Where minor children (under 18 years of age) are playing on a team, their parent or guardian must sign the form on their behalf. 

Identification numbers must be included for every player.  The correct ID number for each category of eligibility is:

·      Current Nortel Networks Employees: Nortel Networks Global ID number

·      Contractors: Contract Number

·      Former employees: 10-digit phone number followed by the designation ‘F’.  This may be a work phone number if the number is listed against the person’s name in the company directory.  Otherwise, use a personal phone number or cell phone number.

·      For employee relatives: Nortel Network employee’s Global ID number, followed by the designation `R'. 

·      For former employee relatives: 10-digit phone number followed by the designation ‘R’. This may be a work phone number if the number is listed against the person’s name in the company directory.  Otherwise, use a personal phone number or cell phone number.

·      For all others: 10-digit phone number followed by designation ‘F’. This may be a work phone number if the number is listed against the peron’s name in the company directory. Otherwise, use a personal phone number or cell phone number.

It is the responsibility of the Captain to ensure that an indemnity form is properly signed and forwarded to the league registrar before the player takes part in a game.

Adding Players to the Team During the Season

Players may be added to the roster as desired until the July 1 deadline.  (The July 1 date is approximate, and the specific deadline for each season will be announced by the League as the season progresses.)   Additions to team rosters during the remainder of the regular season are limited to Spares.  Players who have not played in the league during the current season may be added after the July 1 deadline, with the permission of the Registrar.  Additions to the roster for playoffs are limited to changes allowed under the "The Co-op Rule."

Players Changing Teams

Players may be registered for only one team at a time during the season. 


To change teams, a player must be added to the team's electronic roster via the league's web registration page, and must sign an indemnity form for their new team and forward it to the League Registrar before they play for their new team.  The intent is that a clean transition from old team to new team be observed.

Free Agents/Spares

Spares may be picked up off of the spares list at any time during the regular season.  They must be registered with the league as spares and have signed an indemnity form before they play.  Teams may pick up a spare for a single game or for the remainder of the season.  If a spare is picked up for the remainder of the season, he/she is officially added to the team's roster, is removed from the Spares list and can no longer play for other teams.

Fees

Equipment Deposit

Fees are assessed per team.  (Individual players do not pay fees to the League.)  Team fees are due on equipment pickup day and are payable only by a single cheque or money order (cash is not accepted).  Make cheques payable to Nortel Mixed Softball League.  

Fees change from season to season depending on League expenses and other revenue.  An estimate of the amount of fees is provided at the Annual General Meeting (Captains Meeting), and is confirmed before Equipment Pickup Day. 

Equipment Deposit

Where a team borrows League equipment, a deposit is charged. The deposit amounts for each item will vary with replacement value, and a schedule of deposit amounts will be published by the League at the Annual General Meeting.  Where an equipment deposit is required, the amount is to be added to the Team fees and paid at Equipment Pickup via a single cheque or money order combining both amounts.  Teams are not required to borrow any equipment, and may take only the items they need. 

No deposit is charged for balls, and they do not need to be returned. 

Equipment deposits are refunded at Equipment Return for equipment returned in reasonable condition.  Any team failing to return equipment on or before the designated return date may forfeit part or all of the deposit.

 

Playoff Eligibility

Playoff Eligibility - Players

To be eligible to play for a team in the playoffs, players must have

·       signed the indemnity form for the team they intend to play for in the playoffs before July 1.  Exception:  players who are eligible under "The Co-op Rule" and spares who are eligible under Spares. 

·       played a minimum of five games with the team to be eligible for the playoffs.  

Captains are responsible for determining eligibility against this requirement for their own players.

Playoff Eligibility - Teams

The team must be able to fulfill the league entry requirements with eligible players. (See Roster Requirements ). If a team cannot meet the league entry requirements with eligible players at the end of the season (See Roster Requirements and Playoff Eligibility - Players), they may enter the playoffs with a penalty.

Player Substitution:  "The Co-op Rule"

Teams that have players who are unavailable to play the remainder of the season may replaced by eligible spares on a one-to-one basis at any time before the playoffs, with the following conditions.  The league must approve these substitutions and the new player must sign an indemnity form.  The league maintains the right to disallow any substitution.  The most common reason requiring such a substitution is co-op students returning to school after Labour Day.

Equipment

The League can supply equipment for teams in the softball league.  This equipment is loaned from the league to the teams, and must be returned at the end of the season, or if a team withdraws from active participation during the season.  Each team also receives softballs, one for use at each of their home games.  Softballs do not need to be returned at the end of the season.

Current equipment list:

·       tapered bat

·       bottle-style bat

·       catcher’s mask

·       one set of bases (three bags and a carpet home "plate")

·       equipment bag

·       measuring rope

If the league is using diamonds away from Carling then the following additional equipment will be provided

·       one set of bases (1 double-sized safety base, 2 regular bases and a carpet home plate

Damage

If the equipment is damaged or broken during the season, bring the damaged item to the equipment manager for replacement.  This is the only way to receive replacement equipment from the softball league.

Equipment Pickup and Return

The League specifies a date for Equipment Pickup before the beginning of the season and for Equipment Return after the Playoffs.  Teams are required to send a delegate to Equipment Pickup to pay fees and to receive softballs and any borrowed equipment the team requires.  Teams returning borrowed equipment must send a delegate to Equipment Return to bring back equipment and receive their deposit refund.  The League Executive is not required to offer alternative dates for these transactions. 

Team delegates who do not have access to Nortel Networks buildings must make arrangements with the League Executive in advance for an escort to the Softball Cage.  (Note:  All persons who enter the building with a visitors badge must be escorted at all times.)

Playoffs

Playoff Dates

Playoffs are held over the weekend immediately following Labour Day.  In case of rainout the Playoffs will be played the following weekend.

League Standings

League standings are determined using the following formula:

-       most points

-       least forfeits

-       least no umps

-       who beat who

-       least losses

-       most wins

-       highest sportsmanship rating

-       coin toss
