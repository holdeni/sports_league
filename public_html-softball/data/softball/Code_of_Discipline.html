Introduction

The mandate of the Nortel Networks Mixed Softball League (the Softball League) is to provide a safe and fun place to play recreational softball.  For this reason any incident which involves verbal or physical abuse of others, or shows disrespect for the Softball League or its officers, or volunteers performing duties on behalf of the league, are not tolerated.  Such incidents are taken very seriously and it is the position of the League Executive that the consequences of such behaviour should be serious enough to reflect the extent to which it undermines the mission of the Softball League.

 

This document provides a discipline guide for incidents that may require suspension of participants in the Nortel Networks Mixed Softball League. 

 

This document is owned by the Softball League Executive, and is used by the Executive member (or committee) responsible for discipline to provide guidance and consistency in the determination of consequences for offenses requiring disciplinary action.

Incident Handling Process

Because the operation of the Softball League proceeds without constant oversight by the League Executive, a formal incident process has been defined.  The process allows the gathering of information from participants and witnesses as independently as possible, to provide as accurate a picture as possible of the incident. 

 

Currently, the League Vice President handles incidents as part of his/her normal duties.  These duties may be delegated to another individual temporarily or to another Exec position permanently.  The process description refers to the League Vice President as the Exec member responsible for information gathering, analysis, and rendering of the decision.  Where another Exec member has taken on this duty, the process remains the same.  

 

The Softball League rules and umpiring guidelines require that umpires report to the Softball League Executive any incidents involving ejection of a player from a game or the presence of alcohol at a diamond.  The umpires at the game in question send a report through email.  Email provides written documentation and facilitates timely response. These email are forwarded to the League Vice-President to initiate the investigation process.

 

Where the offence is serious enough to warrant expulsion from the Softball League under any of the clauses below, then the offending participant will be suspended pending the outcome of the investigation process.  For less serious offenses, the offending participant may continue to play pending the outcome of the investigation process.  Every reasonable attempt will be made to gather the required information and deliver a finding before the offending participants next game.  This should be balanced against the need for providing sufficient time for gathering of information from all witnesses and to consider consistencies and inconsistencies in the various reports, and to seek clarifying information as required.  The process should be efficient with a timely delivery of the finding, but should provide for a well-informed and considered opinion. 

 

Once an incident report has been received, a description of the incident is requested from the following:  the offending participant, the umpires the Captain or acting captain of each team present at the game, and anyone else directly involved.  A solid deadline will be provided for return of this input.  Generally this deadline is   no more than two business days.

 

The Vice-President reviews the gathered information, and if further clarifications are required, they will be solicited at that time.  Any unsolicited or late responses will be disregarded and not included in the final decision.

 

A finding will be delivered in a timely manner based on this Code of Discipline.  Generally a decision should be provided within two business days of receipt of all solicited input.

Umpire Abuse

Umpire abuse as defined by (1) repeated or uncontrolled questioning of an umpire's ruling, or (2) repeated heckling of the umpire during play. The umpire shall eject the offender from the game and report the incident to the League Vice President for further disciplinary action.

 

Such misconduct will result in the following additional penalties for the offender:

·        First Offense: Suspension for the next two played games for the players team.

·        Second Offense:  Suspension for the next five games played by the team.

·        Third Offense: Expulsion from the league for at least the remainder of the season.  Reinstatement must be applied for and accepted by the Vice President before the player may participate in a subsequent season.

Verbal Abuse

Verbal Abuse Involving Profanity

Players or other team personnel shall not use profanity directed toward umpires, players or spectators.   The offender shall be immediately ejected from the game.  All such incidents must be reported to League Vice President for further disciplinary action.

 

Such misconduct will result in the following additional penalties for the offender:

·        First Offense: Suspension for the next two games played by the players team. 

·        Second Offense:  Suspension for the next five games played by the players team.

  • Third Offense: Expulsion from the league for at least the remainder of the season.  Reinstatement must be applied for and accepted by the Vice President before the player may participate in a subsequent season.

Verbal Abuse Not Involving Profanity

Players or other team personnel shall not participate in verbal abuse directed toward umpires, players or spectators.  The umpire shall initially warn the offender.  If the abuse continues, or escalates, the offender shall be immediately ejected from the game. All such incidents must be reported to the League Vice President for further disciplinary action.

 

Such misconduct will result in the following penalties for the offender:

·        First Offense: Suspension for the next game played by the players team. 

·        Second Offense:  Suspension for the next four games played by the players team.

  • Third Offense: Expulsion from the league for at least the remainder of the season.  Reinstatement must be applied for and accepted by the Vice President before the player may participate in a subsequent season.

Threatening Physical Violence

Players or other team personnel shall not threaten physical violence directed toward umpires, players or spectators.   Examples of this are challenging persons to a fight, or threatening to hit or punch them or any other verbal threat of physical violence. The offender shall be immediately ejected from the game.  All such incidents must be reported to League Vice President for further disciplinary action.

 

Such misconduct will result in the following additional penalties for the offender:

·        First Offense: Suspension for the next two games played by the players team. 

·        Second Offense:  Suspension for the next five games played by the players team.

  • Third Offense: Expulsion from the league for at least the remainder of the season.  Reinstatement must be applied for and accepted by the Vice President before the player may participate in a subsequent season.

Physical Abuse

Physical abuse by any player directed toward any umpire, player, coach, or spectator will not be tolerated. Any offender directing physical abuse at another person, whether an umpire, any member of another team, or spectator, or a member of his own team, shall be immediately ejected from the game. All such incidents must be reported to the Vice President for further disciplinary action.  

 

Please note that if Corporate Security Guards or City police become involved in any incident below, the participants will be suspended from the league pending the outcome of any legal proceedings.  Any league suspension required under this code will be applied only after completion of any civil or legal proceedings and reinstatement has been requested and received from the League Vice President.

Initiating an Intentional Collision

Under Softball League rules intentional collisions are not tolerated and result in immediate ejection of the offending player. Intentionally is interpreted as; if the runner or fielder is aware of the potential for contact and in the umpires judgment the runner or fielder had the ability to avoid the contact, then a reasonable attempt must be made to avoid the collision. If no attempt is made to avoid the collision, then the collision should be ruled intentional. Any intentional collision resulting in ejection must be reported to the league for further discipline.

 

Such misconduct will result in the following penalties for the offender:

  • First Offense: Suspension for the next game played by the players team. 

·        Second Offense:  Suspension for the next four games played by the players team.

  • Third Offense: Expulsion from the league for at least the remainder of the season.  Reinstatement must be applied for and accepted by the Vice President before the player may participate in a subsequent season.

Initiating a Minor Physical Incident

A minor physical incident is an incident where minor physical contact occurs which is not of a threatening nature.  Examples of this would be minor pushing.  In this case the offender is the person who is first to initiate physical contact.  Please note that this contact does not need to be of an offensive nature.  Where the initial physical contact is of a defensive nature (i.e. restraining an individual to prevent a physical incident), it constitutes an escalation of a previously verbal incident to a physical incident and is punishable under this code. While in most cases participants punished under this code will also be punished under one or more of the codes below, this is not a requirement.  It is possible to initiate contact of a defensive nature, and not participate in the actual physical incident.  This code is intended to punish the person who escalates an incident to a physical incident, whether or not they had offensive intent.

 

Such misconduct will result in the following penalties for the offender:

·        First Offense: Suspension for the next game played by the players team. 

·        Second Offense:  Suspension for the next four games played by the players team.

  • Third Offense: Expulsion from the league for at least the remainder of the season.  Reinstatement must be applied for and accepted by the Vice President before the player may participate in a subsequent season.

Participating in a Minor Physical Incident

A minor physical incident is an incident where minor physical contact occurs which is not of a threatening nature.  Examples of this would be minor pushing, or grabbing.  In this case the offender would be any person who participated as an active participant in the minor physical incident by directing physical actions against others. This code is not intended to punish persons who are on the receiving end of physical abuse, or who are protecting themselves.  It is only intended to punish those persons who direct physical abuse against others.

 

Such misconduct will result in the following penalties for the offender:

·        First Offense: Suspension for the next two games played by the players team. 

·        Second Offense:  Suspension for the next five games played by the players team.

  • Third Offense: Expulsion from the league for at least the remainder of the season.  Reinstatement must be applied for and accepted by the Vice President before the player may participate in a subsequent season.

Initiating a Major Physical Incident

A major physical incident occurs where the physical contact is of a violent or threatening nature.  Examples of this would be major pushing resulting in hard falls, punching, or choking. In this case the offender is the person who initiates the major physical incident or who escalates a minor physical incident to a major physical incident.  This is the person who is first to initiate major physical contact (i.e. the instigator). Initiation of a major physical incident cannot be considered defensive. 

 

Such misconduct will result in the following penalties for the offender:

  • Any Offense: Expulsion from the league for at least the remainder of the season.  Reinstatement must be applied for and accepted by the Vice President before the player may participate in a subsequent season.

Participating in a Major Physical Incident

A major physical incident occurs where the physical contact is of a violent or threatening nature.  Examples of this would be major pushing resulting in hard falls, punching, or choking. In this case we are talking about all persons who participated as active participants in the major physical incident.

 

Such misconduct will result in the following penalties for the offender:

  • Any Offense: Expulsion from the league for at least the remainder of the season.  Reinstatement must be applied for and accepted by the Vice President before the player may participate in a subsequent season.

Participating in an Incident as a Peacemaker

Participating as a peacemaker refers to participating in an incident, whether verbal or physical, with the intent of ending the incident by attempting to calm a member of the opposite team, or by physically moving or restraining a member of the opposite team.   This code is not intended to punish people who attempt to calm or restrain members of their own team. 

 

Such misconduct will result in the following penalties for the offender:

·        First Offense: Suspension for the next game played by the players team. 

·        Second Offense:  Suspension for the next three games played by the players team.

  • Third Offense: Expulsion from the league for at least the remainder of the season.  Reinstatement must be applied for and accepted by the Vice President before the player may participate in a subsequent season.

Other Discipline

Participating in a Subsequent Incident

Participating in a subsequent incident refers to being involved in any subsequent incidents after the incident for which the participant was ejected.  Penalties shall be applied for every subsequent incident in the same game. Note that any penalties incurred as a result of violation of other codes will also be applied to the subsequent incidents. 

 

Such misconduct will result in the following penalties for the offender:

·        First Offense: Suspension for the next two games played by the players team. 

·        Second Offense:  Suspension for the next five games played by the players team.

  • Third Offense: Expulsion from the league for at least the remainder of the season.  Reinstatement must be applied for and accepted by the Vice President before the player may participate in a subsequent season.

Refusing to Leave the Field after Ejection

Once a participant has been ejected from the game, that person must leave the vicinity of the playing field immediately.  The vicinity of the playing field is anywhere in the vicinity of the field where the player can interact with game participants in any manner including but not limited to shouting.  Any player who verbally refuses to leave, or does not remove themselves from the vicinity of the field within one minute of being ejected should be punished under this code.

 

Such misconduct will result in the following penalties for the offender:

·        First Offense: Suspension for the next two games played by the players team. 

·        Second Offense:  Suspension for the next five games played by the players team.

  • Third Offense: Expulsion from the league for at least the remainder of the season.  Reinstatement must be applied for and accepted by the Vice President before the player may participate in a subsequent season.

Returning to the Field after Ejection

This clause applies to a participant who has been ejected from the game, and who has left the vicinity of the playing field, and who returns to the field or vicinity for any reason.  The vicinity of the playing field is anywhere where the player can interact with game participants in any manner including but not limited to shouting.  Any player who returns to the vicinity of the playing field after leaving the vicinity should be punished under this code.

 

Such misconduct will result in the following penalties for the offender:

·        First Offense: Suspension for the next two games played by the players team. 

·        Second Offense:  Suspension for the next five games played by the players team.

  • Third Offense: Expulsion from the league for at least the remainder of the season.  Reinstatement must be applied for and accepted by the Vice President before the player may participate in a subsequent season.

Participating while Suspended

A suspended participant may not participate in their teams games in any manner for the duration of their suspension.  This means the participant may not be present in any area normally considered part of the playing field or its vicinity under this code, during her teams games.  Vicinity is to be interpreted as anywhere within shouting distance of the playing field (approximately 100 metres).

 

Such misconduct will result in the following penalties for the offender:

  • Any Offense: Expulsion from the league for at least the remainder of the season.  Reinstatement must be applied for and accepted by the Vice President before the player may participate in a subsequent season.

Possession of Alcohol at a Diamond

The permits signed by the league specifically state that alcoholic beverages are not permitted at the diamonds or in the vicinity of the diamonds (parking lots, etc). If this part of the permit is violated, we could lose all access to the community diamonds.  For the reason possession of Alcohol at a diamond requires immediate forfeiture of the game, and that the incident be reported to the Softball League for further discipline.

 

Such misconduct will result in the following penalties for the offender and any team Captains present at the game:

·        First Offense: Suspension for the next five games played by the players team. 

  • Any Subsequent Offence: Expulsion from the league for at least the remainder of the season.  Reinstatement must be applied for and accepted by the Vice President before the player may participate in a subsequent season.

General Notes

All suspensions are served consecutively.

 

A suspended participant may not participate in their teams games in any manner for the duration of their suspension.  This means the participant may not be present in any area normally considered part of the playing field, or its vicinity, during her teams games. Vicinity is to be interpreted as anywhere within shouting distance of the playing field (approximately 100 meters).

 

Rained out games and forfeited games do not count towards suspended games. Only games physically played by the players team are counted.

 

Playoff games do not count toward suspended games unless the suspension is incurred in the playoffs.

Appeal Process

Suspensions are appealable to the President of the Softball League. Requests for appeal must be received from the suspended player and one of his team captains via email within two business days of receipt of the original finding. The process followed for this review is identical to the original incident handling process. The Presidents review sets aside the original decision and will be a new review of the incident, with an independent finding. The offending player will take any penalty from this review with no chance for appeal.  If the resulting finding upholds or increases the original suspension, then a further game suspension will be added to the suspension for losing the appeal.
